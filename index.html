ABOUT ME

Hi! I am Kristian Jagoda and am a junior front-end web developer.

I graduated in law from the University of Greenwich in 2011 and I practiced law for over 4 years but realised that it was not for me. I decided to try something different.

One of my friends suggested that my legal skills would lend themselves well with respect to coding and having always been a computer enthusiast I decided to take on that challenge. I started with building simple websites using only HTML and CSS. I then started to learn as well the basics of JavaScript to add some behaviour to my web projects. I found coding challenging but extremely interesting and it quickly became my favourite hobby.

I came to realise that this was something I was good at and wished to take further, further than I could achieve through self-studying. I made the decision to quit my job and enrol on a full-time course. I completed a 7-week course in front-end web development with Coders' Lab in Wroclaw, Poland. This course consisted of 240 hours of lectures and coding.

I have learnt to build solid web foundation by using HTML5, styling it with CSS3 and adding behaviour with JavaScript (and its library – Jquery). I have learned technologies such as Sass and GULP which help to build websites faster and more efficiently. I have also learnt basics of PHP to create my first website in Wordpress.

Coding has become my passion. I understand that my skills might be limited but I am eager to learn and develop them further and my tutors have commended me on how quickly I have learnt the skills mentioned above and I code as often as I can.

Please check some of my work in my portfolio. You can also download my CV ‘here’ or visit my profile on LikedIn.
